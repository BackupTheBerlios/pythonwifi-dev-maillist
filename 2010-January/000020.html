Hi,

> 1) Do you need direct access to freq2channel() and channel2freq() or are
> these just helpers for Wireless.getChannel() and Wireless.setChannel()?  If
> they are not meant to used directly, I think I will put them under the
> Wireless class.

I don't need direct access to these functions. However it would be 
interesting if one could directly convert channels to frequencies and 
vice versa. But since freq2channel() and channel2freq() also need the 
iwrange parameter, I have no objections to putting them under the 
Wireless class.

> 2) The changes to Wireless.setFrequency() are to allow non-string values?
> Is this needed?  Aren't strings good enough?

When I played around with python-wifi it took me some time to figure out 
  which format is supported by the setFrequency() method. Finally I had 
to look in the source code. Therefore the changes should allow to use it 
more intuitively on the one hand. On the other hand, setChannel() needs 
to set the frequency as int or long.

> 3) Iwrange.iwfreqs is a list of frequencies.  Does Iwrange.frequencies not
> serve your needs?

Iwrange.frequencies is a list of frequencies and Iwrange.iwfreqs is a 
list of Iwfreq objects. I need Iwfreq objects because these hold the 
relationship between channel (Iwfreq.index) and frequency 
(Iwfreq.getFrequency())
